# Good Things: 2026-05-28

1. New Zealand Kākāpō Record Breeding Season: The kākāpō, one of the world's rarest birds with just 235 adults, completed its most successful breeding season on record in 2026, with over 100 chicks hatched — a stunning milestone for the long-running conservation program. [https://www.bornfreeusa.org/2026/04/07/defiantly-optimistic-the-recent-wildlife-wins-you-almost-missed/]

2. Superconductivity Record Shattered: Scientists at the University of Houston created a material that conducts electricity with zero resistance at the highest temperature ever achieved under normal pressure conditions, a breakthrough that could transform energy transmission and technology. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/top/science/]

3. Brain Regeneration Compounds Developed: Researchers in Japan created powerful vitamin K-based compounds that may help the brain regenerate lost neurons, offering hope for future treatments for Alzheimer's and Parkinson's diseases. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/health_medicine/]

4. Platypuses Thriving After Reintroduction: Four platypuses reintroduced to Royal National Park in Australia were found to have established a breeding population, with a newly hatched subadult discovered during surveys — a sign the iconic species is returning to its historic range. [https://news.mongabay.com/list/happy-upbeat-environmental/]

5. WHO Honours Primary Healthcare Hero: Dr. Worawit Tontiwattanasap received the UAE Health Foundation Prize at the 79th World Health Assembly for decades of humanitarian work improving healthcare access for stateless, displaced, and undocumented populations in rural communities. [https://www.who.int/news-room/feature-stories/detail/primary-health-care-champions-celebrated-at-the-seventy-ninth-world-health-assembly]
