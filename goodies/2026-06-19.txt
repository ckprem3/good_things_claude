# Good Things: 2026-06-19

1. Colombia Bans FGM: Colombia became the first country in Latin America to pass a law prohibiting female genital mutilation, a historic milestone for women's rights and bodily autonomy across the continent. [https://equalitynow.org/news/news-and-insights/colombia-passes-first-law-in-latin-america-to-eradicate-female-genital-mutilation/]

2. US Drug Overdose Deaths Plunge: CDC data confirmed that drug overdose deaths in the United States fell by nearly 27% in 2024 — from 110,037 to ~80,391 — the largest single-year decline on record, with opioid, cocaine, and methamphetamine deaths all dropping. [https://cdc.gov/nchs/pressroom/releases/20250514.html]

3. São Tomé and Príncipe Creates Marine Sanctuaries: The small West African island nation announced eight new marine protected areas safeguarding coral reefs, sea turtle nesting beaches, and seabird colonies — building on its status as the world's first country to designate its entire territory a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. [https://www.fauna-flora.org/news/minister-announces-eight-new-marine-protected-areas-for-sao-tome-and-principe/]

4. Human Regeneration Ability May Be Dormant, Not Lost: Texas A&M researchers demonstrated that mammals can be prompted to regenerate bone, joints, ligaments, and tendons after amputation using a two-step biological treatment — suggesting that regenerative powers are not lost in humans but simply switched off and waiting to be reactivated. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/06/260617032207.htm]

5. Five "Missing" Bird Species Rediscovered: Five bird species that hadn't been documented in the wild for a decade or more were confirmed to still exist, giving conservationists renewed hope and proof that dedicated search efforts and habitat protection can bring species back from the brink. [https://news.mongabay.com/2026/04/once-lost-now-found-five-missing-bird-species-rediscovered-in-2025-offering-hope/]
