1. Olympian Turns Insult Into Youth Sports Fundraiser: After being called "a real loser" by President Trump, Team USA freestyle skier Hunter Hess launched The Real Loser Project, donating 100% of proceeds to Stoked, a nonprofit giving underserved kids access to action sports and mentorship. [https://www.centraloregondaily.com/news/local/hunter-hess-real-loser-project-youth-sports-fundraiser/article_221413a2-44d9-4865-baa5-554644c856d4.html]

2. Virginia Becomes First Southern State to Guarantee Paid Family Leave: Virginia enacted the 15th paid family and medical leave program in the US, offering workers up to 12 weeks of paid leave to care for a newborn, ill family member, or their own health — a historic first for the South. [https://virginiamercury.com/2026/05/12/virginia-becomes-first-southern-state-to-mandate-paid-family-and-medical-leave-for-workers/]

3. Renewables Beat Natural Gas on US Grid for First Time: In March 2026, wind, solar, hydro, and biomass combined to generate 35% of US electricity, surpassing natural gas at 34% — the first time renewables have topped fossil fuels across an entire month. [https://www.canarymedia.com/articles/clean-energy/renewables-beat-natural-gas-us-grid-march-2026]

4. NYC Turns 50 School Streets Into Soccer Fields for World Cup: Mayor Mamdani's Soccer Streets initiative transformed 50 car-free school blocks across all five boroughs into soccer zones with pickup games, drills, art stations, and block parties, making World Cup joy accessible to every kid in the city. [https://gothamist.com/news/goal-dozens-of-nyc-school-streets-to-host-kids-soccer-activities-ahead-of-world-cup]

5. Single Immunotherapy Infusion May Suppress HIV for Years: Researchers from UCSF reported that two trial participants saw HIV drop to undetectable levels after a single infusion of engineered immune cells, with suppression lasting nearly two years — a major step toward a functional cure. [https://www.lgbtqnation.com/2026/05/new-breakthrough-brings-researchers-closer-to-a-functional-cure-for-hiv/]
