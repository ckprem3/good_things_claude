# Good Things: 2026-06-21

1. Aging Redefined: Yale study finds nearly half of adults 65+ actually improved cognitively and physically over 12 years, challenging the assumption that aging means inevitable decline [https://ysph.yale.edu/news-article/yale-study-challenges-notion-that-aging-means-decline-finds-many-older-adults-improve-over-time/]

2. Alzheimer's Breakthrough: Scientists identified a molecule called OLE that reprograms the brain's immune cells (microglia) to attack toxic plaques, reducing buildup and improving memory in animal models [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/06/260619020506.htm]

3. Nature's Dental Cure: Researchers developed a gel from jackfruit latex, pomegranate peel, and simvastatin that fights gum disease infection, reduces inflammation, and stimulates bone regrowth — a potential game-changer for periodontitis treatment [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/06/260618041508.htm]

4. Back from the Dead: A plant species (Ptilotus senarius) believed extinct for 60 years was suddenly rediscovered, offering hope for conservation efforts and showing nature's resilience [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260517211447.htm]

5. Marathon of Kindness: Boston Marathon runners stopped their own race to help a fellow competitor who had fallen, ensuring she crossed the finish line — a heartwarming reminder of human compassion in sport [https://www.globalpositivenewsnetwork.com/good-news-this-week-june-21st/]
