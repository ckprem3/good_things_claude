# Good Things: 2026-06-02

1. Platypuses Return to Sydney: For the first time in over 50 years, duck-billed platypuses are breeding in Australia's Hacking River near Sydney, with the reintroduced population growing from 17 to 20 animals — a major conservation win. [https://www.positive.news/society/good-news-stories-from-week-22-of-2026/]

2. Renewables Beat Gas: Wind and solar generated more of the world's electricity than gas for the first time ever in April 2026, together accounting for 22% of global electricity vs. 20% from gas — a landmark clean energy milestone. [https://www.positive.news/society/good-news-stories-from-week-22-of-2026/]

3. Girl's Sight Restored by Gene Therapy: Six-year-old Saffie Sandford had her sight restored at London's Great Ormond Street Hospital through "life-changing" gene therapy for Leber's congenital amaurosis, a rare genetic blindness condition. [https://www.positive.news/society/good-news-stories-from-week-22-of-2026/]

4. Wildlife Bridge Cuts Animal Crashes 90%: Colorado completed a major new wildlife overpass over a heavily travelled freeway, designed to let animals safely cross between habitats and expected to reduce animal-vehicle collisions by 90%. [https://earthlinged.substack.com/p/positive-things-are-already-happening]

5. Community Hero Fights for Pets and People: Chantal Wadsworth was named the 2026 More Than a Pet Community Hero for dedicating her life to animal welfare in under-resourced communities, coordinating vet care and personally transporting animals so families never have to choose between their pets and their finances. [https://www.humaneworld.org/en/blog/announcing-2026-more-than-a-pet-community-hero-award-winner]
