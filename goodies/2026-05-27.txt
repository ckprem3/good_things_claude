# Good Things: 2026-05-27

1. Superconductivity Record Smashed: Houston physicists broke a 30-year superconductivity record, achieving zero-resistance electricity conduction at the highest ambient-pressure temperature ever recorded — a leap that could revolutionize energy transmission. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260527023220.htm]

2. Brain-Healing Vitamin K: Scientists created supercharged vitamin K compounds — three times more potent than the natural form — that help the brain grow new neurons, offering hope for future treatments of Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260526233433.htm]

3. French City Champions Its Trees: Sceaux, France unveiled a landmark Tree Charter that legally protects its 65,000+ trees, mandates two new plantings for every tree felled, and offers residents subsidies to plant more — modeling how cities can treat their urban canopy as a long-term investment. [https://reasonstobecheerful.world/sceaux-tree-charter]

4. Zero-Waste Ocean Desalination: Researchers at the University of Rochester unveiled a solar-powered desalination system that converts ocean water into fresh drinking water with no chemical additives and no brine waste — instead extracting nearly 100% of salts in solid, recyclable form. [https://techxplore.com/news/2026-05-solar-powered-desalination-ocean.html]

5. Cincinnati Zoo Conservation Wins: The Cincinnati Zoo released its annual conservation impact report, celebrating the rescue of two dozen turtles from a demolition site, the reintroduction of manatees to the wild, and over 77,000 students educated through wildlife field trips — all powered by community fundraising. [https://spectrumnews1.com/oh/dayton/news/2026/05/27/cincinnati-zoo-touts-achievements-report]
