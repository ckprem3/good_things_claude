# Good Things: 2026-06-24

1. Cape Verde Goalkeeper Reunited With Mom at World Cup: After goalkeeper Vozinha made 7 stunning saves to hold Spain to a 0-0 draw and broke down in tears because his mother couldn't afford to attend, U.S. House Democratic leader Hakeem Jeffries intervened to clear all obstacles — his mother was granted her visa and flew to Miami to watch him play. [https://www.goal.com/en/lists/cape-verde-vozinha-mother-us-visa-world-cup-spain/blt61317a01afadabe2]

2. Scottish Fan Walks 3,000 Miles in a Kilt, Raises £1 Million for Mental Health: 22-year-old Craig Ferguson walked from Los Angeles to Boston — completing a full marathon every day for 104 consecutive days while wearing a kilt — raising over £1 million for Scottish mental health charity SAMH, with Scotland's First Minister adding £400,000 from the government. [https://www.wbur.org/news/2026/06/12/world-cup-boston-scotland-craig-ferguson-trek]

3. Mangroves Reach a Global Turning Point After Decades of Loss: A landmark study using 40 years of satellite data found that mangrove forests worldwide are no longer in net decline, with gains outpacing losses for 16 consecutive years — scientists called it "an important source of optimism for climate action" as these forests protect coastlines and absorb CO2. [https://www.anthropocenemagazine.org/2026/06/mangroves-are-making-a-comeback-its-a-rare-climate-success-story/]

4. New Solar Desalination System Makes Fresh Water With No Toxic Brine: Scientists developed a solar-powered desalination system that converts seawater from three different oceans into drinking water without producing harmful brine, using laser-textured metal panels powered by sunlight that automatically shed salt deposits — with recovered salts potentially usable as a lithium source for batteries. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260530053418.htm]

5. Hero Poodle Saves Owner From House Fire: When a fast-moving fire broke out just before 1 a.m. at Aida Kelly's home in West Milford, New Jersey, her 3.5-year-old poodle Squilliam barked relentlessly until she woke up and escaped safely before the house was engulfed — "He's my hero, my angel," she told CBS News. [https://www.cbsnews.com/newyork/news/west-milford-house-fire-dog-squilliam/]
