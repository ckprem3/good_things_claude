# Good Things: 2026-06-18

1. Forever Chemicals No More: Scientists discovered that hydrogen radicals generated by intense UV light can break down stubborn PFAS "forever chemicals" without any added chemicals, offering a cleaner and cheaper path to removing these pollutants from water supplies. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/health_medicine/]

2. Mangroves Making a Comeback: A major global study found that mangrove gains have begun to outpace losses over the past 16 years, with restoration projects and conservation policies beginning to reverse decades of decline in these vital coastal ecosystems. [https://www.capetownetc.com/conservation/wildlife-conservation-success-stories-june-2026/]

3. Ocean Protected: A new marine protected area covering an ocean region roughly the size of France was announced, delivering a significant boost to ocean conservation and giving marine life a crucial safe haven to recover and thrive. [https://www.euronews.com/2026/05/22/carbon-sucking-fungi-and-forever-chemical-crackdowns-positive-environmental-stories-from-2]

4. A Little Lifting Goes a Long Way: A landmark study tracking more than 147,000 people over 30 years found that just 90–120 minutes of strength training per week delivers some of the biggest long-term health rewards — great news for anyone short on time. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/]

5. Mammals Can Regenerate: Scientists took a surprising step toward unlocking regeneration in mammals, showing that the ability to rebuild complex body parts may not be evolutionarily lost — it may simply be switched off, opening a new frontier in healing and medicine. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/]
