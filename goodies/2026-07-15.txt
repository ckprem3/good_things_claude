# Good Things: 2026-07-15

1. Kākāpō Hit Record 106 Chicks: New Zealand's critically endangered kākāpō parrots completed their most successful breeding season ever, with 106 chicks hatching across three predator-free islands — nearly doubling the previous record of 85 from 2019 [https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/environment/590633/over-100-kakapo-chicks-hatch-in-record-breaking-breeding-season]

2. Ozempic May Slow Biological Aging: A randomized clinical trial published in Nature Communications found semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) slowed epigenetic aging markers by 9% in adults with HIV — the first human trial evidence that a GLP-1 drug may influence how fast people age [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/07/260713084907.htm]

3. NASA Backs 41 Moon & Mars Technologies: NASA selected 41 commercial technology projects to tackle critical challenges for future lunar and Mars missions, covering everything from powering lunar outposts to shielding spacecraft from Moon dust [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/07/260713084855.htm]

4. SpaceX Hits 600th Reused Booster Milestone: SpaceX flew a flight-proven Falcon 9 booster for the 600th time, completing two launches in under 8 hours from opposite US coasts and deploying 56 Starlink satellites, demonstrating reusable rockets are now truly routine [https://www.space.com/space-exploration/launches-spacecraft/spacex-starlink-15-14-b1093-vsfb-ocisly-10-45-b1080-ccsfs-asog]

5. Wind & Solar Overtake Gas Globally: For the first time in history, wind and solar generated more electricity than gas worldwide (22% vs 20%), producing a record 531 TWh in April 2026 — a milestone that would have seemed impossible just five years ago when gas generated nearly double what renewables did [https://ember-energy.org/latest-updates/for-the-first-time-wind-and-solar-generated-more-electricity-than-gas-worldwide-in-april-2026/]
