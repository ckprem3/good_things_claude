# Good Things: 2026-07-12

1. Kākāpō Record Breeding Season: New Zealand's critically rare kākāpō parrot smashed its previous breeding record in 2026, with over 100 chicks hatching — up from the previous best of 85 in 2019 — offering real hope for the species with only 235 adults remaining. [https://www.enviroblog.net/post/three-wildlife-conservation-stories-define-2026-two-triumphs-and-a-hard-won-stabilization]

2. Sea Turtles Rebounding Worldwide: Green sea turtles have made such a remarkable recovery thanks to decades of conservation efforts that their status was officially downgraded from "endangered" to "least concern," a stunning turnaround for a species once on the brink. [https://www.enviroblog.net/post/three-wildlife-conservation-stories-define-2026-two-triumphs-and-a-hard-won-stabilization]

3. Bacterium Wipes Out Cancer Tumors: Scientists discovered that a naturally occurring bacterium found in amphibian intestines completely eliminated colorectal tumors in mice with a single treatment, both attacking cancer cells and activating the immune system — a major step toward new cancer therapies. [https://www.positive.news/society/good-news-stories-from-week-27-of-2026/]

4. Sorority Raises $125 Million for St. Jude: Tri Delta, a college sorority with chapters across the US and Canada, hit a landmark $125 million fundraising milestone for St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, helping fund life-saving research for children with cancer. [https://www.sunnyskyz.com/good-news]

5. Weekly Climate Hope — July 12: This week's climate optimism roundup highlighted clean energy milestones and conservation wins, including Ethiopia's campaign to plant 700 million trees a day and Bangladesh slashing all tariffs on electric vehicles and solar power to accelerate its green transition. [https://climateactapp.substack.com/p/your-weekly-dose-of-climate-hope-43f]
