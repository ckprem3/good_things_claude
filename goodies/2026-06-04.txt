# Good Things: 2026-06-04

1. Pancreatic Cancer Breakthrough: A new drug called daraxonrasib nearly doubles survival rates (from 6.7 to 13.2 months) for patients with advanced pancreatic cancer, targeting the previously "undruggable" KRAS mutation and reducing risk of death by 60% in a Phase 3 trial of 500 patients. [https://www.cbsnews.com/news/pancreatic-cancer-treatment-daraxonrasib-survival-rate/]

2. GLP-1 Drugs Fight Addiction: A landmark study of over 600,000 U.S. veterans found that popular weight-loss drugs like Ozempic reduce addiction rates across all major substances — overdoses dropped ~39%, drug-related deaths fell ~50%, and suicidal ideation decreased 25%. [https://www.science.org/content/article/obesity-drugs-linked-lower-addiction-rates-large-study-veterans]

3. Zero-Waste Solar Desalination: University of Rochester scientists unveiled a solar-powered desalination system that converts seawater into fresh drinking water with zero toxic brine waste, while also recovering valuable minerals like lithium — successfully tested with water from three oceans. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260530053418.htm]

4. Kākāpō Record Breeding Season: New Zealand's critically endangered kākāpō parrot (only 235 adults exist) completed its most successful breeding season ever, with over 100 chicks hatching — a major win for one of the world's rarest and most beloved birds. [https://www.bornfreeusa.org/2026/04/07/defiantly-optimistic-the-recent-wildlife-wins-you-almost-missed/]

5. North America's Largest Wildlife Crossing Opens: A sweeping wildlife overpass spanning six lanes of I-25 in Colorado opened, reconnecting fragmented habitats for elk, deer, antelope and other animals — the largest such crossing on the continent and a model for coexistence with nature. [https://www.bornfreeusa.org/2026/04/07/defiantly-optimistic-the-recent-wildlife-wins-you-almost-missed/]
