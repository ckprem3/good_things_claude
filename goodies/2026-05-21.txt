# Good Things: 2026-05-21

1. TB Treatment Saves 83 Million Lives: The World Health Assembly met on May 21 to endorse a new post-2030 tuberculosis strategy, celebrating that expanded TB treatment saved an estimated 83 million lives between 2000 and 2024 — with 2024 marking the first post-pandemic decline in cases. [https://www.who.int/news/item/21-05-2026-seventy-ninth-world-health-assembly---daily-update--21-may-2026]

2. Endangered Species Day Celebrates Wildlife Wins: Conservationists marked Endangered Species Day 2026 by highlighting remarkable recovery stories — the bald eagle rebounded from under 500 breeding pairs to roughly 14,000, gray wolves now roam California in 10 packs, and the American alligator has recovered to over 5 million individuals. [https://www.earth.com/news/endangered-species-day-2026-celebrating-wildlife-recovery-stories/]

3. James Webb Telescope Maps the Cosmic Web: Astronomers using NASA's James Webb Space Telescope created the clearest map ever of the universe's "cosmic web" — the enormous hidden structure of filaments and voids that connects galaxies across billions of light-years of space, offering a stunning new window into the cosmos. [https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/top/science/]

4. Crocheter Completes 100th Handmade Baby Blanket: A woman in England reached a heartwarming milestone by crocheting her 100th handmade blanket for newborn babies, a long-running gesture of gratitude toward the hospital that once saved her sight — each blanket given freely to a new family starting their journey. [https://www.goodnewsnetwork.org/]

5. Coho Salmon Return to California Rivers: Conservation efforts paid off as Coho salmon — absent for decades — returned to California's Russian River and other habitats, a sign that river restoration and dam removal projects are helping ecosystems recover and bringing hope to communities and wildlife alike. [https://news.mongabay.com/short-article/2026/05/endangered-species-day-highlights-wildlife-wins-and-mounting-losses/]
