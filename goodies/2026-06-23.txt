# Good Things: 2026-06-23

1. Bipartisan Housing Bill Passes Senate 85-5: The US Senate overwhelmingly passed the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act, a sweeping bipartisan bill aimed at increasing housing supply, lowering costs, and limiting Wall Street from buying single-family homes — a rare show of unity in a gridlocked Congress. [https://thehill.com/homenews/senate/5935003-housing-affordability-bill-senate/]

2. NASA's Quantum Lab Upgrade Unlocks New Frontier: NASA's upgraded Cold Atom Lab aboard the International Space Station is now producing Bose-Einstein condensates five times larger than ever before, extending quantum observation windows that gravity makes impossible on Earth and opening doors to new space-based discoveries. [https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/nasas-quantum-lab-aboard-space-station-gets-chilly-upgrade/]

3. Europe Breaks Dam Removal Record, Freeing 3,740 km of Rivers: A record 603 river barriers were removed across Europe in 2025, reconnecting over 3,740 kilometers of waterways and restoring vital habitats for fish and wildlife — with 29 countries now participating in the river restoration movement. [https://europe.wetlands.org/new-record-for-dam-removal-in-europe-with-over-600-barriers-dismantled/]

4. Goalkeeper's Mother Crosses the World to Watch Her Son Play: Cape Verde goalkeeper Vozinha's mother, Ana Candida Evora, made her first-ever international trip to watch her son play in the FIFA World Cup 2026 — after a viral story, diplomatic intervention, and 24+ hours of travel from Cape Verde finally got her there. [https://abcnews.com/Sports/wireStory/faith-vozinhas-mom-speaks-cape-verde-heads-back-134075499]

5. Japan's First New Bird Species in 40 Years Identified: Scientists formally described the Tokara Leaf Warbler (Phylloscopus tokaraensis), the first new bird species found in Japan in over four decades, discovered by analyzing DNA and birdsong on the remote Tokara Archipelago. [https://a-z-animals.com/articles/scientists-just-discovered-japans-first-new-bird-species-in-over-40-years/]
