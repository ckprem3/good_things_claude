# Good Things: 2026-05-17

1. Kākāpō Hits Record Breeding Season: New Zealand's critically endangered kākāpō parrot — with only ~235 adults alive — hatched over 100 chicks in 2026, smashing the previous record of 85 from 2019 and giving conservationists enormous hope for the species. [https://www.earth.com/news/endangered-species-day-2026-celebrating-wildlife-recovery-stories/]

2. Renewables Beat Natural Gas on the US Grid for the First Time: In March 2026, wind, solar, hydropower, and biomass collectively generated more electricity than natural gas in the United States — a historic first — with renewables supplying over a third of the country's power. [https://www.canarymedia.com/articles/clean-energy/renewables-beat-natural-gas-us-grid-march-2026]

3. NASA's Artemis II Crew Returns as Record-Breakers: The four Artemis II astronauts — Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen — completed a historic crewed lunar flyby, traveling 695,081 miles and setting the record for the farthest any humans have ever ventured from Earth, with Koch becoming the first woman and Hansen the first non-American to reach the Moon's vicinity. [https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-welcomes-record-setting-artemis-ii-moonfarers-back-to-earth/]

4. Japan's Red-Crowned Crane Removed from Threatened Species List: Once feared extinct in the early 1900s with fewer than 20 birds remaining, Japan's iconic red-crowned crane has rebounded to nearly 2,000 individuals through decades of dedicated conservation, earning its formal removal from Japan's threatened species list. [https://www.euronews.com/2026/03/12/carbon-sucking-fungi-and-forever-chemical-crackdowns-positive-environmental-stories-from-2]

5. V&A East Museum Opens Free to the Public in London: A spectacular new branch of the Victoria and Albert Museum opened in London's Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park, offering free permanent galleries shaped with input from local young people and bringing world-class culture to one of the city's most diverse communities. [https://www.timeout.com/london/news/the-v-a-east-will-open-this-weekend-heres-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-major-new-london-museum-041526]
