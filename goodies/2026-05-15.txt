# Good Things: 2026-05-15

1. NASA's Next-Gen Space Processor: A palm-sized chip 500x more powerful than current spacecraft computers passed testing, paving the way for autonomous deep-space missions to the Moon and Mars [https://www.nasa.gov/technology/hello-universe-nasas-next-gen-space-processor-undergoes-testing/]

2. Cartilage Regeneration Breakthrough: Scientists found that blocking the protein 15-PGDH reverses cartilage loss in aging joints, with human tissue from knee-replacement patients already showing new, functional tissue growth — potentially ending the need for knee replacements [https://www.goodnewsnetwork.org/anti-aging-drug-regrows-knee-cartilage-in-major-breakthrough-that-could-end-knee-replacements/]

3. Optimism Cuts Dementia Risk: A study of over 9,000 people tracked for 14 years found that higher optimism is linked to a 15% lower risk of developing dementia, with the protective effect holding across different demographics [https://neurosciencenews.com/optimism-dementia-risk-prevention30477/]

4. Thailand's "Last Titan" Dinosaur: Scientists identified Nagatitan chaiyaphumensis, Southeast Asia's largest-ever known dinosaur — a 27-tonne, 27-metre-long sauropod from 113 million years ago — reshaping our picture of prehistoric Asian wildlife [https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/5/15/scientists-identify-massive-new-dinosaur-following-thailand-dig]

5. US Road Deaths Near Historic Low: The US Department of Transportation confirmed that traffic fatalities fell 6.7% in 2025 to 36,640 — the second-lowest total and fatality rate in over 104 years of record-keeping, continuing a four-year downward trend [https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/traffic-deaths-2025-early-estimates-2024-annual]
