# Good Things: 2026-06-07

1. Bumblebees Solve Problems Like Chimps: A landmark study published in Science showed bumblebees spontaneously used tools to reach an out-of-reach reward — the first time this has ever been demonstrated in any invertebrate, challenging the assumption that insight-level problem-solving requires a large brain. [https://www.npr.org/2026/06/07/nx-s1-5846947/bumblebees-problem-solving-research]

2. Teen Inventors Win Earth Prize for Microplastic Miracle: Three 16-year-old students from India — Vivaan Chhawchharia, Ariana Agarwal, and Avyana Mehta — won the Asia regional Earth Prize for "Plas-Stick," a magnetic tamarind-seed powder that clumps invisible microplastics so they can be removed from water with a simple handheld magnet, requiring no electricity. [https://www.goodnewsnetwork.org/3-teens-win-earth-prize-for-asia-inventing-tamarind-powder-that-easily-removes-microplastics/]

3. 18 Rescuers Spend 6 Hours Freeing Dog From Underground Sinkhole: Maisy, a 12-year-old Staffordshire bull terrier, fell 21 feet into a narrow fissure on the North York Moors. The Scarborough and Ryedale Mountain Rescue Team mobilised 18 volunteers who formed a human chain underground to pass her to safety — and Maisy was returned to her relieved owner unhurt. [https://www.goodnewspost.co.uk/dog-rescued-after-falling-20ft-underground-in-north-york-moors/]

4. One Million People Fill Madrid's Streets to Welcome Pope Leo XIV: An estimated one million people gathered at the Plaza de Cibeles and surrounding streets for a Corpus Christi Mass celebrated by Pope Leo XIV, bringing a spirit of unity and joy to Spain's capital in what organisers called the largest public gathering in Madrid in years. [https://www.npr.org/2026/06/07/nx-s1-5849147/1-million-flood-madrid-streets-pope-procession]

5. Eight Nurses Graduate at Bermuda College: Eight individuals completed their associate degrees in nursing at Bermuda College, joining the frontline healthcare workforce — a small but meaningful milestone celebrated by their families and the community they will serve.
