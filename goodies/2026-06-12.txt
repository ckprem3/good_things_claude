# Good Things: 2026-06-12

Teen Heroes on a Bike Ride: Two 13-year-old Iowa boys, Gunner Skidmore and Kohen Chick, spotted an elderly neighbor who had fallen and been stranded in her yard for over 16 hours without water or her phone — their quick action and call for help saved her life. [https://www.sunnyskyz.com/good-news/6221/Two-Teen-Out-for-a-Summer-Bike-Ride-End-Up-Saving-Elderly-Woman-Trapped-Outside-for-16-Hours]

NICU Nurses Write a Coloring Book for Siblings: Connecticut Children's Hospital NICU nurses spent years creating "A Message to My Sibling," a coloring book helping brothers and sisters understand, feel included, and stay connected to newborns receiving intensive care. [https://www.sunnyskyz.com/good-news/6220/NICU-Nurses-Create-Children-039-s-Book-to-Help-Siblings-Stay-Connected-to-Hospitalized-Babies]

Rare Bongos Caught on Camera in Kenya: Trail cameras captured three critically endangered mountain bongos — including a young male and female — in Kenya's Maasai Mau forest where the species had not been confirmed for years, giving conservationists renewed hope. [https://www.goodnewsnetwork.org/incredibly-rare-bongos-caught-on-trail-cam-in-area-they-were-thought-to-be-extinct/]

4. Anti-Aging Drug Regrows Knee Cartilage: Stanford Medicine researchers found that blocking an aging-related protein restored lost cartilage in old mice and prevented arthritis after injuries — and human joint tissue responded too, potentially making knee replacements unnecessary in the future. [https://www.goodnewsnetwork.org/anti-aging-drug-regrows-knee-cartilage-in-major-breakthrough-that-could-end-knee-replacements/]

5. Baby Coyote Rescued from Hundreds of Cactus Spines: A 4-week-old coyote pup found alone in an Arizona yard, covered head-to-tail in barbed cholla cactus spines, was rescued by the Southwest Wildlife Conservation Center — after two hours of careful removal, the pup is recovering and will eventually be released into the wild. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/2026/06/10/baby-coyote-trapped-hundreds-cactus-spines-is-freed-by-rescuers/]
