# Good Things: 2026-06-26

1. HPV Vaccine Eliminates Cervical Cancer Deaths in Young Women: For the first time ever, no women in their early 20s died from cervical cancer in England between 2020 and 2024, with scientists saying the risk for vaccinated girls is now "effectively zero" — a triumph of preventive medicine. [https://fortune.com/2026/06/23/cervical-cancer-deaths-hit-zero-england-rfk-jr-vaccine-hpv/]

2. Our Ocean Conference Raises $6.4 Billion for Marine Protection: The 11th Our Ocean Conference in Mombasa, Kenya, concluded with $6.4 billion in pledges to protect corals, coasts, and whales, including the largest single ocean pledge ever — €3.5 billion from the EU. [https://news.mongabay.com/2026/06/our-ocean-conference-in-kenya-ends-with-6-4-billion-in-pledges-review-of-past-promises/]

3. UK Electric Vehicles Outsell Petrol Cars for the First Time: In the 12 months to May 2026, UK consumers bought 516,490 new electric vehicles versus 504,010 new petrol cars — a historic milestone marking the first full year where EVs outsold petrol cars in Britain. [https://www.carbonbrief.org/analysis-uk-sales-of-electric-vehicles-just-overtook-petrol-cars-for-the-first-time/]

4. Beavers Solve Flooding Crisis at London Tube Station: A family of five reintroduced beavers in a west London nature reserve built dams that stopped Greenford Tube station from flooding after heavy rain — a low-cost, nature-based solution that also brought back 8 new bird species, 2 bat species, and rare butterflies. [https://www.positive.news/environment/how-beavers-solved-a-flooding-problem-in-west-london/]

5. Teen Beats Stage 4 Cancer and Walks at Graduation: Dylan Mwaniki, diagnosed with aggressive Stage 4 kidney cancer at 14 and given just 8 months to live, completed treatment and graduated high school — reuniting with his surgeon Dr. Mary Austin, who kept her promise to be there on his big day. [https://www.cbsnews.com/news/dylan-mwaniki-stage-4-cancer-friendship-mary-austin/]
